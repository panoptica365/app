The tenant dashboard

Ten tabs, two zones: the metric cards, the drill-down panels, and where each workflow lives.

Click a tenant on the Main Console and you land on its dashboard. This is the single richest page in Panoptica365 — everything known about one tenant, organized into ten tabs:

  1. Overview — the configuration and activity snapshot (this guide).
  2. Trends — this tenant's posture over time: Secure Score, score by category, issues caught and resolved (own guide, Trend dashboards).
  3. Alerts — this tenant's alerts, same workflow as the global Alerts page (own guide, Work the alerts).
  4. Security — the tenant-wide security-settings surface: dozens of monitored switches across M365, Entra, Exchange, Teams and SharePoint, read and guarded for drift (own guide, Monitor security settings).
  5. Access Review — who holds admin roles, which accounts are dormant, and the break-glass workflow (own guide, Access review).
  6. Applications — the enterprise app inventory and approval workflow (own guide, Review applications).
  7. Email Auth — each domain's email-authentication posture: SPF, DKIM and DMARC, graded and watched for drift (own guide, Email authentication).
  8. CA Policies — Conditional Access templates assigned to this tenant (own guide, Deploy Conditional Access policies).
  9. Intune Policies — Intune templates deployed to this tenant (own guide, Deploy Intune policies).
  10. Change Log — the history of every change made to this tenant, both changes Panoptica365 made (deployments, applies) and changes operators logged manually. Drift alerts link back to entries here when a change explains them.

The Overview tab: metric cards

The top zone is a grid of at-a-glance cards. What appears depends on what the tenant has, but expect:

  • Secure Score — with the comparative average for similar-sized tenants.
  • Identity: Total Users, Licensed Users, Global Admins (green at 2 or fewer, red above 5 — count matters), MFA Registration percentage (green at 90%+), Risky Users, Inactive Users (90d).
  • Access control: Conditional Access Policies (enabled/disabled split), Security Defaults on/off.
  • Devices: Compliant Devices percentage with a trend arrow, Inactive Devices (90d), Entra Connect sync status.
  • Collaboration: SharePoint Sites, Anonymous Links (high severity if any exist), OneDrive Accounts, Teams (public/private split).
  • Email: Mailboxes, 7-day Mail Activity, Inbox Rules — with an external-forwarding indicator, one of the most common compromise signals.
  • Apps and DNS: Registered Apps, Enterprise Apps, Domains with MX/SPF/DMARC/Autodiscover validation status.

Treat the cards as a triage surface: anything red or yellow is a question worth answering.

The Overview tab: drill-down panels

Below the cards, collapsible panels carry the detail behind each card: the licensing breakdown, the actual list of global admins, users without MFA, per-policy CA details, the full Intune device table, Top Users by Storage and Top Mailboxes by Storage (shown in GB), anonymous sharing links by site, all inbox rules grouped by user, inactive user and device lists, registered and third-party apps, and DNS records per domain.

You'll use these panels constantly during assessments and customer conversations — "you have four global admins and two of them are unlicensed accounts nobody owns" comes straight from here.

SharePoint: storage and audits

The SharePoint drill-down has its own tabs — Inventory, Library Permissions, User Permissions, Storage Overview, and Audits. Storage Overview is per-site: each site is listed once with its real storage (in GB) and its library count, so the totals add up instead of repeating a site's storage on every library.

The Audits tab runs a SharePoint permissions audit and tracks it as a background job. Launch one per library, per site ("audit all libraries in this site"), or across the whole tenant with Audit All Sites — confirmations are tiered, and the all-sites one warns that it's heavy and can hit Microsoft Graph throttling. Because audits are tracked jobs, launching one no longer navigates you away: the Audits tab lists every job as running, queued, done, or failed; you can cancel queued ones and re-run failed ones, and the inventory carries a Last Audit Date column.

Data freshness

Everything on the Overview reflects the last poll (the interval you set per tenant, 1–60 minutes, plus slower cycles for heavyweight data). If you've just onboarded the tenant, give the first poll a few minutes; if a card seems stale, check Last Polled on the Tenants page.

Where to next

A sensible first pass over a freshly onboarded tenant: skim the Overview for anything alarming, then work through Applications (approve what you trust), then CA Policies and Intune Policies (deploy your baselines), then Security (the tenant-wide settings surface, its own tab).
