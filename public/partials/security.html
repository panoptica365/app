Turn on Standard protection in Microsoft Defender

This tenant has never had Microsoft's Standard preset security policy turned on. Microsoft creates the underlying policies only the first time the preset is enabled in the Defender portal — there is no API or PowerShell command that can create them from scratch. So this first activation is a one-time manual step. Once it's done, Panoptica takes over and monitors it for drift automatically — you'll never have to do this again for this tenant.

Keep the Standard preset rather than building custom policies: Microsoft re-tunes these policies daily from billions of threat signals, so the customer's protection keeps improving on its own.

Step 1 — Open the preset policies

Go to security.microsoft.com and sign in to the customer's tenant. In the left nav: Email & collaboration → Policies & rules → Threat policies. Under Templated policies, open Preset Security Policies. On the Standard protection card, click Manage protection settings to launch the “Apply standard protection” wizard.

Step 2 — Exchange Online Protection

Anti-spam, anti-malware and anti-phishing for inbound mail. Set Apply protection to → All recipients and leave Exclude these recipients unchecked. Protect the whole organization, not a subset. Click Next.

Step 3 — Defender for Office 365 protection

Safe Attachments and Safe Links (included with Business Premium). Again set Apply protection to → All recipients and leave the exclusions unchecked. Click Next.

Step 4 — Impersonation protection: people

Add the high-value people most likely to be spoofed in phishing / business-email-compromise attacks — not everyone. Good candidates:

  • CEO, owner or managing director
  • CFO or finance director, and anyone who can authorize payments
  • HR director (payroll and direct-deposit targets)
  • Any other executive or signatory with payment or data authority

For each person, enter their display name and primary email, then Add (for example, John Smith — john.smith@domain.com). Adding low-risk staff just inflates false positives, so keep this list to the handful of high-value targets. Click Next.

Step 5 — Impersonation protection: domains

Add the customer's own email domain(s) here — for example domain.com, plus any other domains they send mail from. This flags external senders trying to impersonate the customer's own domain. Click Next.

Step 6 — Trusted senders and domains

Leave this empty for now. This is the allow-list of senders/domains you never want flagged as impersonation — start clean. If a legitimate partner later gets caught, come back and add them here. Click Next.

Step 7 — Policy mode

Select Turn on the policy when finished, then click Next.

Step 8 — Review and confirm

Review the summary and click Confirm. Microsoft now creates the Standard preset policies and turns them on for all users.

Step 9 — Hand it back to Panoptica

Come back here and click Refresh now below (or close and use the page's Refresh button). Panoptica will re-read the tenant and detect the now-live preset. Because you added impersonation targets, the live config will differ from the old baseline — open this setting again and click Accept This Change to adopt the live Microsoft preset as your monitored baseline. From that point on, Panoptica monitors it for any deviation.

Keep the Standard preset rather than building custom policies: Microsoft re-tunes these policies daily from billions of threat signals, so the customer's protection keeps improving on its own.

Step 1 — Open the preset policies

Go to security.microsoft.com and sign in to the customer's tenant. In the left nav: Email & collaboration → Policies & rules → Threat policies. Under Templated policies, open Preset Security Policies. Slide the Standard protection toggle to On, then click Manage protection settings to start the wizard.

Step 2 — Exchange Online Protection

This is the only configuration page you'll see on this licence. Set Apply protection to → All recipients and leave Exclude these recipients unchecked. Protect the whole organization, not a subset. Click Next.

Step 3 — Straight to Review

On this licence the wizard goes straight to the Review page — there is no Defender for Office 365 step and no impersonation pages (those need Business Premium). That's expected, not a problem.

Step 4 — Review and confirm

Make sure the Standard protection toggle is On, review the summary and click Confirm. Microsoft creates the Standard preset's EOP policies and turns them on for all users.

Step 5 — Hand it back to Panoptica

Come back here and click Refresh now below (or close and use the page's Refresh button). Panoptica will re-read the tenant and detect the now-live preset. If it shows as drift against the old baseline, click Accept This Change to adopt the live config as your monitored baseline. From that point on, Panoptica monitors it for any deviation. When the customer later moves to Business Premium, re-run this on the Defender for Office 365 and impersonation steps to complete the hardening.
